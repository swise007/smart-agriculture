Tomato Early Blight

94% Confidence

Disease Information

Early blight is a common fungal disease that affects tomatoes, potatoes, and other plants. It is caused by the fungus Alternaria solani and typically appears as concentric rings on leaves, resembling a target.

Recommended Treatment

  • Apply copper-based fungicides every 7-10 days
  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts
  • Use chlorothalonil or mancozeb fungicides
  • Apply neem oil as an organic alternative

Prevention Tips

  • Practice crop rotation (3-4 year cycle)
  • Ensure proper plant spacing for air circulation
  • Water at the base, avoid overhead irrigation
  • Use disease-resistant varieties when possible

Best Practices

  • Monitor plants regularly for early signs
  • Maintain soil fertility with balanced fertilization
  • Remove plant debris at the end of the season
  • Use mulch to prevent soil splashing onto leaves
